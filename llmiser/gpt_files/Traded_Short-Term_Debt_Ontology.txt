
Bankers' acceptance is defined as short-term debt instrument that is guaranteed and paid by a bank and used as a relatively safe form of payment for large transactions. Bankers' acceptance is a kind of bill of exchange. It has guarantor some bank. It has relative price at issue value at a discount. It has relative price at maturity value par value.
Bill of exchange is defined as short-term negotiable financial instrument consisting of an order in writing addressed by one person (the seller of goods) to another (the buyer), requiring the latter to pay a fixed amount of money on demand (a sight draft) or on a predetermined date (a time draft). Bill of exchange has synonyms bank draft  draft. Bill of exchange is a kind of money market instrument. It has party in role at least zero payee. It has party in role some drawee. It has party in role some drawer. It is issued by at least zero financial institution.
Commercial paper is defined as an unsecured short-term debt instrument typically issued by a bank, corporation, or foreign government to obtain funds to meet short-term debt obligations, such as accounts receivable, inventories, or payroll, backed only by an issuing bank or company promise to pay the face amount on the maturity date specified on the note. Commercial paper is a kind of money market instrument. It has relative price at issue value at a discount. It has relative price at maturity value par value.
Drawee is defined as party that is required to pay the amount stated on the bill of exchange to the payee. Drawee is a kind of payer. It is played by some that is a party to some bill of exchange.
Drawer is defined as party that requires a drawee to pay either a third party or themselves with respect to a bill of exchange. Drawer is a kind of obligee. It is played by some that is a party to some bill of exchange.
Eurodollar deposit is defined as a certificate of deposit with a fixed interest rate issued in U.S. dollars outside the jurisdiction of the Federal Reserve, held at banks outside of the United States, including branches of U.S. banks located outside of the U.S.. Eurodollar deposit is a kind of certificate of deposit.
Money market instrument is defined as a short-term debt security that gives the owner the unconditional right to receive a stated, fixed sum of money on a specified date. Money market instrument is a kind of fixed income security.
Repurchase agreement is defined as agreement between two parties whereby one party lends the other a security at a specified price with a commitment to take the security back at a later date for another specified price. Repurchase agreement is a kind of money market instrument, mutual contractual agreement. It has duration at most one duration.
